About me

From culinary to coding

I was in high school when I decided I wanted to pursue a career in the culinary arts and I couldn't have been more happy when I got accepted into the Art Institute of Pittsburgh. So, in the Summer of 2013, I was finally able to pursue my life-long passion of becoming a chef. As my second semester began, labs started that kept me locked in a kitchen for 5 or 6 hours at a time. It became uneventful and my passion started to die. I dropped out only 3 semesters in. It was not right for me. I started working at a pizzaria, then a car dealership. I was fulfilling my financial needs but I was miserable. Somewhere along the way, I met my husband, Mike. He is an inspiration to me and so supportive of my happiness. One day we were talking about careers and he suggested web development might be a good match for me and I laughed at him. A couple days went by, then I decided to look into coding as a career. While researching, I found the passion I'd been missing.

To me, full-stack web development is a perfectly balanced dish. It blends my unique and burning passions for mathematics, design, and problem-solving. I view each and every project the same way I approach cooking a meal. As I continue my education through Lambda School, I add more "ingredients" into my pantry, and find excitement in combining those ingredients to create customized solutions to the problems proposed to me. Coding is a science. It combines an intense mastery of computing logic and a willingness and discipline to follow the scientific method. Coding is also an art. It requires a person to think freely and to be willing to explore outside the confines of conventional approaches. To me, coding and cooking go hand in hand.
